# BPL Room Booking

> Note: This case study documents a real professional project completed during **2023** at **Beximco Pharmaceuticals Ltd.** The original source code is private due to company confidentiality and ownership restrictions.

## Overview

BPL Room Booking was an internal web application developed to support room booking, resource scheduling, and administrative management workflows within an organizational environment.

I worked on this project in **2023** as the **team lead**, together with **two other team members**.

The system was designed to digitize booking operations, improve scheduling visibility, and provide structured control over users, roles, departments, locations, and resources. It included both end-user and administrative workflows, making it more than a simple reservation tool. It was a real internal business application built to support operational coordination.

## Business Problem

Manual booking and scheduling processes often create inefficiency, poor visibility, and confusion around availability, ownership, and approval flow.

This project addressed problems such as:
- inefficient manual room booking processes
- limited visibility into booking schedules and availability
- difficulty managing users, departments, and roles in a structured way
- lack of centralized control over locations and bookable resources
- the need for both user-side and admin-side booking workflows
- the need for maintainable internal tooling for operational coordination

## Users

The main users of the system were:
- **Internal employees or users**, who needed to create and manage room bookings
- **Administrative users**, who managed bookings, users, roles, resources, departments, and system settings
- **Operational stakeholders**, who needed structured scheduling and resource visibility
